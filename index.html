POETRY IS A BEAUTIFUL LITERARY ART FORM, WHICH IS EITHER READ AS TEXT OR WATCHED AS A PERFORMANCE.

MARSHALL JONES’ SATIRICAL SLAM POEM ‘TOUCHSCREEN’ IS ONE SUCH ‘PERFORMED’ POEM ABOUT OUR DIGITAL IMMERSION.

WHICHEVER FORM IT TAKES, THERE’S ALWAYS AN AIR OF

‘PLEASE STAND BEHIND THE YELLOW LINE’

WHEN IT COMES TO POETRY.

THERE IS A DISTANCE OF SEPARATION BETWEEN THE ART AND THE AUDIENCE.

BUT SOME RULES ARE MEANT TO BE BROKEN

IN THE SPIRIT OF BREAKING THE RULES OF ART PERCEPTION,
I DREW INSPIRATION FROM 20TH CENTURY ARTIST AND
ALL-ROUND REBEL MARCEL DUCHAMP; THE MAN WHO
CHALLENGED THE BOUNDARIES OF WHAT WAS ACCEPTED AS ART
BY CREATING ‘READYMADES’ OR ‘ANTI-ART’ ARTWORK CREATED
BY REPURPOSING EVERYDAY OBJECTS. HIS RATIONALE WAS THAT
THE ‘ART’ LIES IN DECONSTRUCTING THE MEANING OF A WORK
AND THE AESTHETIC IS SIMPLY A BYPRODUCT.

THE RESULT OF THIS EXPLORATION IS A WEB TOY:

SCATTERED AROUND A VIRTUAL DESKTOP.

STANZA TO STANZA, COLLAGING THE POEM
